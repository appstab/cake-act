PART 1 - PRELIMINARY

An ACT to set out the responsibilities of employees of
Appstablishment
to provide cakes

[Assented to 1993]
  1. This Act may be cited as the Cake Act 1993
DEFINITIONS
  1. For the purposes of this act an employee is defined as:
    1. A person employed in a full-time, part-time or casual capacity by
      Appstablishment.
    2. A person engaged in a contract for provision of labour to the above mentioned company.
  2. This act covers current and future employees of Appstablishment.

Definitions of 'cake' and 'approved substitute' under the Act.

  1. For the purposes of this Act, 'cake' is henceforth defined as: a sweet, usually
    unleavened bread with other ingredients besides flour, e.g. currants, spices, eggs, sugar; or a quantity of this baked in thick disk or ornamental shape.
  2. For the purposes of the operation of this Act, the 'approved substitutes' for 'cake' are:
    1. deluxe biscuits;
    2. slices, (savoury or sweet);
    3. a generous proportion of party-pies, sausage rolls, quiches and cocktail frankfurts, hors d'oeuvres ; and,
    4. other food combinations as approved in advance by the Minister for Cake, or approved delegate.

OPERATION OF THE CAKE ACT 1993
  1. An employee's obligation to provide cakes or approved substitutes falls due on the following occasions:
    1. Upon the day of that employee's birthday, subject to the following caveats:
      1. if the above mentioned employee was born on the 29th of February, cakes fall due on the closest available working day should it not be a leap year;
      2. if the above mentioned employee's date of birth falls on a non-working day, cakes fall due on the closest available working day; and,
      3. if the above mentioned employee's date of birth falls during holidays, cakes fall due on the closest available working day.
    1. for employees receiving appointments or promotions, either within or outside the Appstablishment, cakes fall due on the closest available working day after confirmation of that appointment or promotion.
    2. cakes fall due upon resignation from the Appstablishment;
    3. cake fall due upon purchase of a new car, bicycle or as such;
    4. Person not getting killed qualifies as cake;
    5. cake fall due upon purchase / sell house;
    6. cake fall due upon marriage / divorce;
    7. cake fall due upon partner getting Visa approved.
PENALTIES
  1. Non adherence to the terms set out under the Cake Act 1993 will be punished by:
    1. a requirement for provision of cakes or approved substitutes for morning and afternoon tea the next working day; or,
    2. flogging.

ACTS AMENDMENT (CAKE)
ACT
An ACT to amend certain Acts in consequence of the enactment of the Cake Act 1993
[Assented to 1994]

PART 1 - PRELIMINARY

Short Title

  1. This Act may be cited as the Acts Amendment (Cake Act) 1994.

Commencement

  1. This Act shall come into operation on the 1 January 1994.

PART 2 - CONSEQUENTIAL AMENDMENTS

  1. Section 6 of the principal Act is amended -
    1. by adding the subsection following -
      1. In the case of more than one employee having an obligation to provide cake on any one day, the following caveats apply:
        1. the approved delegate for the Minister for Cake shall indicate a new date of cake obligation, so that only one employee provides cake on any day; or,
        1. if authorized by the approved delegate for the Minister for Cake, there can be provision for a 'special feast day' whereby a co-ordinated provision of cake and cake substitute is allowed.
